# Executive summary

**Shieldbreak:** Treg Depletion and/or Inhibition
**Target effect:** Reduce regulatory T cells in humans — by absolute depletion, ratio shift toward effectors, or Treg-phenotype destabilization — across tumor, tumor-draining lymph node, blood, bone marrow, and involved tissue.
**Evidence base:** 51 trial rows across 44 unique studies, with 44/44 (100%) skeptic-critiqued; mix of full-text PMC, paywalled-via-WebFetch HTML, and PubMed-abstract-only sources, no retractions.

## What this report covers

The prescriber synthesized 51 trial rows into 6 ranked intervention classes plus 7 unranked counterexamples, weighted by skeptic confidence, risk-of-bias, and counter-productive-mechanism (CP-MoA) severity. This PDF reproduces the per-intervention narrative and per-trial efficacy/toxicity tables verbatim, prefaced by this summary.

## Top-line findings

- **Treg-depletion magnitude is frequently uncoupled from clinical benefit in this dataset**, and several of the apparently-positive classes carry structural confounds (CD25-gating in denileukin diftitox; frequency-vs-absolute-count framing in IL-2 variants).
- **Anti-CCR4 (mogamulizumab) is the most-replicated, mechanism-coherent depletion** — but the same surface-marker promiscuity that enables Treg ADCC also depletes CCR4+ CD8 effector-memory cells (paper-internal in Fujikawa 2023; CP severity High).
- **Standard anti-CTLA-4 does not deplete intratumoral Tregs in humans** — Sharma 2019, Huang 2011, and Penter 2023 converge on FoxP3+ *increase*. Fc engineering (Rank 2) is the reconciling variable.
- **The bempegaldesleukin PIVOT-IO phase 3 program failed (2022)**, disconfirming the "favorable-ratio" non-α IL-2 variant framing; nemvaleukin ARTISTRY-7 also failed (2023).

## Ranked interventions (summary)

1. **Anti-CCR4 (mogamulizumab)** — Strongest, most-replicated depletion with direct tumor evidence; CP **High** (CCR4+ CD8 effector-memory collateral).
2. **Fc-enhanced anti-CTLA-4 (BMS-986218, botensilimab)** — Mechanism-rescue for standard anti-CTLA-4; thin but directionally clean; CP **Moderate** (tdLN Treg expansion, ADCC on activated effectors).
3. **Low-dose metronomic cyclophosphamide** — Schedule-dependent (positive metronomic, null single-IV); cleanest dose-selective mechanism in the set; CP **Low** metronomic, Moderate single-IV.
4. **Denileukin diftitox (DD / ONTAK / E7777)** — Largest literature, but CD25-gating confound and Attia-vs-Dannull split make it ambiguous; CP **Moderate** (CD25+ effector collateral).
5. **Class-I HDAC inhibitors (entinostat / vorinostat / panobinostat)** — Plausibly favorable in oncology, opposite-direction in HIV/cART; CP **Moderate**.
6. **Standard anti-CTLA-4 (ipilimumab / tremelimumab)** — Foundational negative result; CP **Low** (proximal mechanism fails so collateral is moot).

Counterexamples surveyed but not ranked: non-α IL-2 variants (PIVOT-IO failed), anti-CD25 daclizumab (CD25-gating confound), iberdomide / CELMoDs (wrong direction), DNMTi monotherapy, anti-GITR, anti-TIGIT, PI3Kδ inhibitors.

## What this report does *not* cover

Individual patient outcomes, regulatory or commercial analysis, comparative cost, or guidance for clinical decision-making. The frame is research planning: which mechanisms are worth pursuing, with what readouts, and at what level of confidence.

## How to use this report

Each ranked intervention has a per-trial detail table with quantitative efficacy and toxicity. Cells marked `Unknown - non-OA` indicate the source paper is paywalled and data may exist in inaccessible full text. CP-MoA severity is distinct from patient-level toxicity — a severe counter-productive mechanism can pull an overall rating down even when the proximal endpoint is met.
