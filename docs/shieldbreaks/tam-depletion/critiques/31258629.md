## Key critique

Phase Ib of pexidartinib + paclitaxel in advanced solid tumors. PBMC monocyte PD is the readout in the row; the paper reports expected CSF1R class PD (monocyte drop, CSF1 rise) alongside paclitaxel-typical AEs. Modest clinical activity.

## Per-dimension findings

<details class="sb-section" open markdown="1">
<summary>Show / hide</summary>


### Design

Phase Ib dose-escalation (3+3) with expansion, pexidartinib 600–1000 mg BID + weekly paclitaxel 80 mg/m², n=38 treated. Open-label, single-arm, NCT01525602.

### Sample size

38 dosed; PD cohort smaller.

### Effect magnitude calibration

Class-consistent PBMC monocyte reduction alongside paclitaxel-typical response rate.

### Missing data

Standard phase 1 attrition; full-text details available.

### Multiplicity

Phase 1 — descriptive.

### Generalizability

Heavily pretreated solid tumors; safety-dominant study.

### Gating / definition

Flow cytometry on circulating monocyte subsets; full panel in Methods.

### Counter-productive mechanisms

Pexidartinib + paclitaxel stacks lymphopenia and neutropenia risks. Pexidartinib's KIT/FLT3 activity extends the off-target footprint beyond CSF1R. Hepatotoxicity (black-box) means some patients discontinue at doses that would maintain target engagement.

### Conflict of interest & funding

Daiichi Sankyo funded; industry co-authors disclosed.

### Spin / framing

No substantive spin — conclusions match results.

</details>
