## Key critique

<details class="sb-section" open markdown="1">
<summary>Show / hide</summary>


Neoadjuvant sotigalimab + chemoradiation in locally advanced PDAC. Paired tumor biopsies with scRNA-seq. Shows CD40-agonist-driven remodeling of myeloid compartment — proinflammatory shift. Sotigalimab's parent program (PRINCE phase 2) was mixed — didn't reach PFS benefit overall.

</details>

## Per-dimension findings

<details class="sb-section" open markdown="1">
<summary>Show / hide</summary>


### Design

Neoadjuvant window-of-opportunity study, paired biopsies pre- and post-sotigalimab + CRT.

### Sample size

Small neoadjuvant cohort.

### Effect magnitude calibration

Myeloid remodeling signal; clinical benefit framed within LA-PDAC resectability improvement.

### Missing data

Reported.

### Multiplicity

Translational — exploratory.

### Generalizability

LA-PDAC — specific population.

### Gating / definition

scRNA-seq myeloid clusters with multi-marker TAM subtypes. Strong gating.

### Counter-productive mechanisms

Same CD40-agonism class collateral: CRS, transaminitis, thromboembolism. Combined with chemoradiation, lymphopenia is stacked. Radiation can further confound TAM analysis (radiation-induced myeloid infiltration).

### Conflict of interest & funding

Apexigen sponsored; academic co-authors.

### Spin / framing

Measured.

</details>
