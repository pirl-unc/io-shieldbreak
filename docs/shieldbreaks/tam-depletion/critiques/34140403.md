## Key critique

<details class="sb-section" open markdown="1">
<summary>Show / hide</summary>


Randomized phase 1 of APX005M (sotigalimab, CD40 agonist) + cabiralizumab (anti-CSF1R) ± nivolumab in advanced PDAC (NCT03214250). Negative efficacy (ORR ~0%); PBMC PD confirms target engagement. This trial is one of the clearest examples in the set of 'target hit, tumor unmoved' — important for the overall skepticism calibration on CSF1R-based combinations in PDAC.

</details>

## Per-dimension findings

<details class="sb-section" open markdown="1">
<summary>Show / hide</summary>


### Design

Randomized phase 1, two arms (triplet vs doublet), PDAC post-first-line chemotherapy. n=28 treated across arms.

### Sample size

Small phase 1 with randomization to explore safety of triplet vs doublet; not powered for efficacy.

### Effect magnitude calibration

Effectively null response signal — consistent with the cabiralizumab + nivolumab PDAC phase 2 failure (Wainberg 2021).

### Missing data

Reported per protocol.

### Multiplicity

Two arms, descriptive.

### Generalizability

Second-line PDAC — a setting where nothing has worked in TAM-modulation trials.

### Gating / definition

PBMC flow cytometry for monocyte subsets; limited paired tumor biopsies.

### Counter-productive mechanisms

Triple immune modulation (CSF1R + CD40 + PD-1) risks over-activating systemic immunity without productive tumor-antigen engagement. CSF1R blockade may remove antigen-presenting macrophages that CD40 agonism is trying to activate — a plausible mechanistic conflict. PDAC's desmoplasia and low TMB compound the challenge.

### Conflict of interest & funding

Apexigen (APX005M), Five Prime (cabiralizumab), BMS (nivolumab) — three-sponsor trial.

### Spin / framing

Honest about lack of clinical activity.

</details>
