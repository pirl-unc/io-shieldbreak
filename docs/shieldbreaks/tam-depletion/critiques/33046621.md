## Key critique

<details class="sb-section" open markdown="1">
<summary>Show / hide</summary>


Phase 1b AMG 820 + pembrolizumab in advanced solid tumors. Two rows in the table: PBMC-early and tumor-mid. PBMC readout is class-typical (monocyte reduction, CSF1 rise); tumor TAM data are limited to small paired-biopsy subsets. ORRs are low (<10%) across expansion cohorts (PDAC, NSCLC, CRC).

## Per-dimension findings

<details class="sb-section" open markdown="1">
<summary>Show / hide</summary>


### Design

Phase 1b, 3+3 dose-escalation AMG 820 1100–2100 mg q3w + pembrolizumab 200 mg q3w; expansion cohorts in PDAC, NSCLC, CRC. Open-label, single-arm, NCT02713529.

### Sample size

n=56 overall; paired-biopsy subsets smaller.

### Effect magnitude calibration

1/19 NSCLC PR (5.3%) — essentially background for pembrolizumab-experienced disease. Consistent with the null-combo pattern across anti-CSF1R/anti-PD-(L)1 trials except NSCLC in the Gomez-Roca paper.

### Missing data

Paired-biopsy cohort definition in Methods; discussed via Fig numbers.

### Multiplicity

Multiple expansion cohorts; descriptive reporting.

### Generalizability

PDAC/NSCLC/CRC — three tumor contexts with different TAM biology, pooled for PBMC PD and split for efficacy.

### Gating / definition

Blood monocyte subsets by flow; tumor CD68/CD163 IHC on paired biopsies.

### Counter-productive mechanisms

Combination with pembrolizumab: the AE profile includes the CSF1R class signals (periorbital edema, LFT elevation) plus immune-related AEs from pembrolizumab. The Gomez-Roca analysis elsewhere in this set suggests deep TAM depletion may remove beneficial antigen-presenting TAMs; the low ORR here is at least consistent with that hypothesis.

### Conflict of interest & funding

Amgen / Merck co-sponsored; standard industry disclosures.

### Spin / framing

Reasonable — negative efficacy is reported as such.

</details>
