# Executive summary

**Shieldbreak:** Tumor-associated Macrophage Depletion, Inhibition, or Repolarization
**Target effect:** Durable human tumor-compartment TAM modulation, captured orthogonally by intent (intervention class) and observation (absolute reduction, ratio shift, repolarization, or functional impairment). CPI monotherapy and healthy-volunteer PD are out of scope.
**Evidence base:** 37 trial rows across 35 unique PMIDs, 35 skeptic critiques (100% coverage; 24/35 PMC full-text); CP-severity distribution 6 High / 25 Moderate / 4 Unknown / 0 Low.

## What this report covers

The prescriber's synthesis of four ranked TAM-targeting intervention classes plus five unranked observations, anchored to per-trial efficacy and toxicity tables. The PDF carries the cross-cutting caveat, ranked prioritization table, and source list verbatim.

## Top-line findings

- The single most consequential paper in the set — Gomez-Roca 2022 (PMID 35577503, n=221, skeptic-High confidence) — reports paper-internally that **deeper CSF1R+/CD163+ depletion is associated with worse outcomes**, and persistence of a residual TAM subpopulation tracks with CD8 infiltration and benefit. This reframes "maximal depletion" as the wrong PD endpoint.
- **Zero Low-CP-severity trials** in the set: no ranked intervention is free of a structural counter-productive concern. Rankings are relative, not absolute endorsements.
- The CD40-agonist + CSF1R combination is **null-efficacy** in the available evidence (Weiss 2021, Machiels 2020 — both 0% ORR) and the failure is mechanism-coherent: CSF1R depletion removes the macrophage population CD40 is trying to activate. A specific research-planning contraindication.
- CCR2/CCR5 blockade is **effectively program-failed** for oncology TAM indications: Noel 2020 was terminated for pulmonary toxicity, and Grierson 2025 contains an unusual sponsor-acknowledged underperformance concession. All four CD47/SIRPα papers are hematologic — solid-tumor TAM-modulation evidence is absent.

## Ranked interventions (summary)

1. **CSF1R pathway (mAb + TKI)** — largest reproducible PD signal; CP severity **High** driven by Gomez-Roca paper-internal inverse correlation between depletion depth and CD8 benefit.
2. **CD40 agonism** — non-redundant repolarization-without-depletion mechanism; CP severity **Moderate**; avoid CSF1R combos.
3. **CD47 / SIRPα blockade** — strong hematologic-malignancy efficacy but TAM biology is inferred (no phagocytosis assay) and two phase 3 trials (ENHANCE, ENHANCE-3) failed post-dataset; CP severity **Moderate-High**.
4. **CCR2 / CCR5 blockade** — class effectively program-failed; CP severity **High**; retained for the Nywening 2016 mechanism-hypothesis value.

## What this report does *not* cover

Individual patient outcomes, regulatory or commercial analysis, cost, or CPI monotherapy. The frame is research planning. Five additional classes (CLEVER-1, LILRB2/TREM2, PI3Kγ, trabectedin/lurbinectedin, STING) are described as unranked observations rather than ranked interventions, due to thin or program-attenuated evidence.

## How to use this report

Each ranked intervention has a per-trial detail table with quantitative efficacy and toxicity for every trial considered. Cells marked `Unknown - non-OA` indicate the source paper is paywalled and the data may exist in inaccessible full text. The Ranked prioritization table summarizes Likelihood of desired effect, Toxicity burden, and Counter-productive MoA columns at the class level.
