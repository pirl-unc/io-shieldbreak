## Key critique

<details class="sb-section" open markdown="1">
<summary>Show / hide</summary>


MARIO-3 translational phase 2 of eganelisib + atezolizumab + nab-paclitaxel in front-line TNBC. Paired biopsies demonstrate TAM reduction and M1 shift with CD8+ T-cell influx. Clinical outcomes of the larger MARIO-3 program have been disappointing and eganelisib's development has effectively halted — important context for this row.

</details>

## Per-dimension findings

<details class="sb-section" open markdown="1">
<summary>Show / hide</summary>


### Design

Translational analysis within MARIO-3 (NCT03961698), exploratory endpoints on paired biopsies.

### Sample size

Paired-biopsy cohort small; PD analysis hypothesis-generating.

### Effect magnitude calibration

TAM reduction + M1 shift is consistent with the drug's preclinical profile. Clinical activity: the parent MARIO-3 trial's overall efficacy has not translated to a registrational signal.

### Missing data

Paired-biopsy attrition typical.

### Multiplicity

Multiple translational endpoints; exploratory.

### Generalizability

Front-line metastatic TNBC — well-defined indication.

### Gating / definition

mIF / scRNA-seq of tumor biopsies; multi-marker TAM definition.

### Counter-productive mechanisms

Same PI3Kγ class collateral as Hong 2023 — neutrophil/DC dysfunction, autoimmune-like hepatitis risk. Combining with chemotherapy (nab-paclitaxel) adds lymphopenia that could blunt the CD8+ influx the drug is trying to produce.

### Conflict of interest & funding

Infinity / Arcus sponsorship.

### Spin / framing

Translational paper — measured claims.

</details>
