## Per-dimension findings

<details class="sb-section" open markdown="1">
<summary>Show / hide</summary>


### Design

Phase 1 window-of-opportunity trial of neoadjuvant mogamulizumab + nivolumab prior to tumor resection. n=16 evaluable. Paired tumor biopsies and scRNA-seq.

### Sample size

n=16 is small but paired pre/post-resection tumor tissue provides high-quality compartment-specific data.

### Effect magnitude calibration

Median 86.7% CCR4+ eTreg reduction in tumor; all 16/16 evaluable depleted at the eTreg level. Total FOXP3+ mixed (8/16 decreased). The eTreg-specific depletion is the mechanistic signal; total FOXP3+ heterogeneity reflects that anti-CCR4 depletes the CCR4+ subset but not all Tregs. Orthogonally corroborates Fujikawa 2023 blood data (PBMC eTreg ~90% depletion).

### Missing data

Small cohort; paired design.

### Multiplicity

Multi-subset analyses; not explicitly corrected. The primary endpoint (eTreg depletion) had unanimous directional finding (16/16).

### Generalizability

Resectable solid tumors; specific tumor types to be confirmed. Neoadjuvant window-of-opportunity design is specific.

### Treg gating / definition

CCR4+FoxP3+ effector-Treg gating (Miyara-style). High-quality gating.

### Counter-productive mechanisms

Mogamulizumab + nivolumab neoadjuvant in operable solid tumors. Class counter-productive mechanism from Fujikawa 2023 and the preclinical Tanaka 2021 Nature Communications paper applies directly [external]: CCR4 is expressed on central memory CD8 T cells and Th1 effectors, so anti-CCR4 ADCC depletes beneficial effectors alongside Tregs. The paper reports high-grade lymphopenia consistent with this. Severity is high — the mechanism is well-evidenced across the anti-CCR4 class and is not mitigated by the neoadjuvant setting.

### Conflict of interest & funding

Kyowa Kirin / Ono (Japan) sponsorship context.

### Spin / framing

Appropriate: eTreg depletion framed as the mechanistic readout, not the clinical benefit claim.

</details>
